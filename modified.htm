Fifth Beatle items fail at auction
Sutcliffe [centre] was a founder member of The Beatles
Personal possessions belonging to "fifth Beatle" Stuart Sutcliffe have failed to sell at auction in London.

Sutcliffe, who died of a brain haemorrhage in 1962 at the age of 21, was credited with giving The Beatles their distinctive image.

Many items, including photographs, letters and his birth certificate - which had been estimated to fetch up to £8,000 - failed to find buyers.

However, a 1967 art sketch book did sell for £3,000 at Bonhams.

Sister's dedication

The belongings were put up for auction by Sutcliffe's sister Pauline.

She has dedicated her life to collecting and writing about her brother, who was one of the original members of the Liverpool group.

The bass guitarist was a founding member of the Quarrymen, who later became The Beatles, but he quit in 1961, before the band hit the big time, to pursue a career in art.

The 1993 film Backbeat, starring Stephen Dorff, told the story of Sutcliffe's life.
